## Root Cause Analysis

### Bug 1: TestPrioritizeTasks (internal/mcp)
- **Symptom:** t1 (blocking task) not ranked first as expected
- **Root cause:** t3's age was set to 30 days (`now.AddDate(0, -1, 0)`), giving it an age score of 1.0 (weight 1.5, contribution 1.5). Combined with better effort_fit (0.7 vs 0.4 for t1), t3's total score equaled or exceeded t1's blocking bonus (weight 3.0, value 0.333, contribution 1.0). The scoring formula: `(weightedSum / totalWeight) * 100` produced a tie at ~36.5 for both t1 and t3.
- **Fix:** Reduced t3's age from 30 days to 5 days. At 5 days, t3's age contribution drops to 0.25, making t1's blocking bonus clearly dominant (t1 ≈ 36.5 vs t3 ≈ 24.0).

### Bug 2: TestOfflineManager_ConnectivityState (internal/sync)
- **Symptom:** "open is offline" subtest gets "probing" instead of "offline"
- **Root cause:** All three subtests shared a `ProbeInterval: time.Millisecond` config. The circuit breaker's `State()` method calls `checkTransitions()`, which transitions from Open → HalfOpen when `time.Since(lastTripped) >= probeInterval`. With a 1ms probe interval, the circuit transitioned to half-open before `ConnectivityState()` could observe the Open state.
- **Fix:** Made probe interval per-case: `time.Hour` for the "open" case (stays open), `time.Millisecond` for the "half-open" case (transitions quickly).
